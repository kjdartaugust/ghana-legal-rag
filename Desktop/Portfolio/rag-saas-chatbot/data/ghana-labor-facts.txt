GHANA LABOUR FACTS — SUPPLEMENTARY REFERENCE
=============================================

MINIMUM WAGE
------------
Ghana's National Daily Minimum Wage is set annually by the National Tripartite Committee (NTC) under the framework of the Labour Act 2003 (Act 651), Section 112.

- 2024 National Daily Minimum Wage: GHS 18.15 per day
- 2025 National Daily Minimum Wage: GHS 19.97 per day
- The wage is set by the Fair Wages and Salaries Commission after negotiation between the government, employers (Ghana Employers' Association), and workers (TUC Ghana).
- Employers are required by law to pay at least the national minimum wage to all workers.
- Source: Fair Wages and Salaries Commission / Labour Act 2003 (Act 651), Section 112.

ANNUAL LEAVE
------------
- Every worker is entitled to at least 15 working days of paid annual leave after 12 months of continuous service (Labour Act 2003, Act 651, Section 20).
- A worker and employer may agree to postpone annual leave but it cannot be waived.

WORKING HOURS
-------------
- Normal working hours must not exceed 8 hours per day or 40 hours per week (Labour Act 2003, Act 651, Section 33).
- Any work beyond this is overtime and must be paid at a higher rate.

NOTICE PERIOD FOR TERMINATION
------------------------------
- Monthly paid worker: at least one month's notice or one month's pay in lieu.
- Weekly paid worker: at least one week's notice or one week's pay in lieu.
- Source: Labour Act 2003 (Act 651), Section 17.

MATERNITY LEAVE
---------------
- Female workers are entitled to at least 12 weeks (3 months) of maternity leave with full pay (Labour Act 2003, Act 651, Section 57).
- A pregnant worker cannot be dismissed on grounds of pregnancy.

SICK LEAVE
----------
- Workers are entitled to paid sick leave as agreed in the employment contract, with a minimum as prescribed under the Labour Act 2003.

CHILD LABOUR
------------
- The minimum age for employment is 15 years (Labour Act 2003, Act 651, Section 58).
- Children under 18 cannot be employed in hazardous work.

COMPANY REGISTRATION (COMPANIES ACT 2019, ACT 992)
---------------------------------------------------
- A private company requires a minimum of one shareholder and one director.
- Registration is done through the Registrar General's Department (RGD).
- A company must have a registered office in Ghana.
- Private companies cannot offer shares to the general public.
- Source: Companies Act 2019 (Act 992).
